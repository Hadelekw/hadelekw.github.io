Personal information

My name is Maksymilian Wiekiera. I have recently graduated from University in Zielona Góra in Computer Science with the title of Master of Engineering. Previously I have acquired the title of Bachelor of Engineering in Geoinformatics and Satellite Techniques at the same university.

During my studies I have developed variety of interdisciplinary interests. I work in-between the branches of information theory, applied mathematics, Earth sciences, and urbanism.

Currently I am in the process of preparing a publication with my supervisor from University of Zielona Góra, prof. Andrzej Obuchowicz, on optimization of traffic flow in collision-free intersection systems using metaheuristics and an algorithm based on tropical algebra. This project was also the basis of my master's thesis.

Research interests

The list presented below is not an exhaustive one.

  • Geographic Information Systems (GIS): development of tools and spatial data analysis; automated cartography; geomatics; spatial network building; automated satellite imaginery analysis.

  • Intelligent Transport Systems (ITS): traffic simulation; cellular automata as ITS; analysis of stochastic agent behavior; optimization of transport using ITS.

  • Computer vision: analysis of satellite imaginery; photogrammetry; automated object detection using machine learning (supervised and unsupervised).

  • Data analysis and machine learning: interpretable neural networks; unsupervised machine learning.

Projects

  • MPlusA (documentation) is a small Python library for calculations in tropical algebra.

  • GLiss (Green Light initial state searcher) is the project utilizing simulated annealing and tropical algebra in order to achieve as short as possible of a travel time for a vehicle travelling through a collision-free intersection system.
